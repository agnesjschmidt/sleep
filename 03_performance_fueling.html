Nutrition & Stimulant Timing

What you eat is
secondary.
When you eat
is the variable.

Founders optimize almost everything before they get to nutrition. And when they do, they optimize the wrong variable. Whether to eat protein or carbs matters less than whether you've eaten at all — and far less than when relative to your cognitive windows, exercise, and sleep.

The brain is an organ with specific metabolic demands. It runs on glucose. It requires specific precursors for neurotransmitter synthesis. It is brutally sensitive to timing — eat at the wrong time and you blunt the cortisol peak that drives morning focus, spike insulin mid-morning and create a 2-hour cognitive hole, or consume caffeine at the wrong phase and actively suppress your adenosine clearance.

This isn't about a diet. It's about understanding food, caffeine, and hydration as inputs to a biological performance system — and timing them to support rather than undermine the work you're trying to do.

The interventions in this module require no supplements, no special diet, no calorie counting. They require only a new relationship to when. Most founders see measurable improvement in sustained focus and crash reduction within 5–7 days of applying even a single timing change from this module.

Question 01

When do you have your first caffeine of the day?

Cortisol peaks 30–60 min after waking. Caffeine before this peak blunts it and creates dependency. After the peak, it amplifies alertness effectively.

Question 02

When is your last caffeine of the day?

Caffeine half-life is 5–7h. Late caffeine suppresses adenosine — your sleep pressure signal. This directly impacts deep sleep quality.

Question 03

How do you approach eating before or during deep work blocks?

High-glycaemic meals create insulin spikes followed by cognitive dips. Fasted or low-carb states support sustained focus for most people.

Question 04

Steady energy = good metabolic fueling. Peaks and crashes = blood sugar instability, often from meal timing or composition.

Protocol A

Caffeine Architecture

The single highest-ROI fueling change available. Restructuring when — not how much — you take caffeine directly improves morning cortisol response, afternoon energy stability, and sleep quality simultaneously. Duration: 7 days to feel the difference.

1

Delay first caffeine by 90 minutes from waking

Cortisol is your natural stimulant. It peaks sharply 30–60 minutes after waking and clears by ~90 minutes. Caffeine during this cortisol peak is biochemically redundant — you're adding stimulation on top of maximum natural stimulation. It also suppresses the cortisol response itself, meaning you become dependent on caffeine to achieve what your body would do naturally. Wait 90 minutes. The first 3 days feel awful. From day 4 onward, you will notice distinctly cleaner, more sustained morning energy.

Daily — from day 1
2

Hard cutoff: no caffeine after 1pm

With a 5–7h half-life, a coffee at 2pm means you have 50% of that caffeine active at 7–9pm. This is not speculation — this suppresses slow-wave sleep measurably even when you don't feel stimulated and fall asleep easily. The sleep deprivation it creates then demands more caffeine the next day. Breaking this cycle requires 5–7 days of discomfort, after which most founders report they no longer need afternoon caffeine because their sleep quality has improved enough.

Daily — hard cutoff
3

Caffeine with or after food — not fasted on empty stomach

Fasted caffeine spikes cortisol more aggressively and increases anxiety response. Taking caffeine with or 15–20 minutes after a small amount of food moderates the cortisol spike, reduces caffeine anxiety, and produces a smoother, more sustained effect. Even a small amount of protein (handful of nuts, eggs) is sufficient. This also prevents the nausea and anxiety some people experience from black coffee on an empty stomach.

Daily — with/after food
4

Cap at 200–400mg total daily caffeine

200mg ≈ 2 standard espresso shots. 400mg is the evidence-based upper limit for cognitive benefit without anxiety/cortisol dysregulation in most people. Above 400mg: diminishing returns + cortisol dysregulation + sleep degradation. Founders who drink 5–6+ coffees daily are typically masking poor sleep with stimulants while degrading the sleep quality that would make the stimulants unnecessary. Reducing dose while fixing timing is almost always more effective than maintaining high dose with poor timing.

Daily maximum
Protocol B

Pre-Work Fueling

For founders who eat before deep work and notice dips, or who skip eating entirely and experience early focus degradation. This protocol optimizes the 2-hour window before a deep work block.

1

Identify your optimal pre-deep-work state: fasted vs. light fuel

About 30% of people focus optimally in a fully fasted state. 70% need some fuel but perform worse with a full meal. Test this deliberately over 5 days: 2 days fasted, 2 days with a small protein/fat meal (eggs, nuts, Greek yogurt), 1 day with a carb meal. Score your focus quality each session. Your biology will give you a clear answer. Do not assume — test.

5-day personal trial
2

If eating before deep work: protein + fat, low carb

Protein and fat do not create insulin spikes. Carbohydrates — especially refined — do. An insulin spike creates a compensatory glucose drop 60–90 minutes later, which is your focus dip. Before deep work: eggs, Greek yogurt, nuts, avocado — anything in the protein/fat category. If you want carbs, add them after your peak window, not before. This single change eliminates the mid-morning crash for most people who experience it.

Pre-deep-work — every session
3

Allow 60–90 min between eating and starting deep work

The digestive process diverts blood flow and triggers a mild post-prandial state that reduces cognitive performance for 30–90 minutes after eating. Eating immediately before starting work means you begin in a physiologically suboptimal state. Build a gap. If time is the constraint: eat a smaller amount to reduce the digestive load, or eat during a low-cognitive window and protect the post-meal time for admin.

Pre-deep-work timing rule
Protocol C

Crash Elimination Protocol

For founders who experience significant post-meal dips or afternoon crashes severe enough to interrupt work. This is almost always a meal composition and timing problem, not a biology problem.

1

Reduce carbohydrate load at lunch dramatically

The post-lunch dip is primarily caused by a large carbohydrate load at midday. A meal high in refined carbs (rice, bread, pasta, noodles) spikes blood glucose then triggers a compensatory drop that creates the crash. Replace: protein + vegetables + healthy fat at lunch. If this is culturally or practically difficult (e.g. Korean business lunches), reduce the rice/carb portion by 50% and add protein. The effect is measurable within 2–3 days.

Daily — at lunch
2

Add a brief walk after lunch

10–15 min of light walking after eating significantly blunts the post-meal glucose spike and speeds metabolic clearance. This is one of the most evidence-backed post-meal interventions for energy stability and insulin sensitivity. A walk around the block after lunch is not a wellness habit — it is a direct blood glucose management tool. Athletes use it. You can too.

Daily — post-lunch
3

If crashing: strategic caffeine — but only if timing is right

A small amount of caffeine (50–100mg) consumed at the onset of the post-lunch circadian dip (around 1–2pm) can sustain afternoon performance — IF your last caffeine is before 1pm total. This is not a recommendation to add caffeine; it is a precise use of caffeine at the optimal biological timing window. Do not use this as an excuse to have caffeine later in the day — the timing must remain before 1pm total cutoff.

Strategic — max before 1pm
4

Track blood glucose response if crashes persist

If significant crashes continue after meal composition changes: use a continuous glucose monitor (CGM) for 2 weeks (e.g. Freestyle Libre — available without prescription in most markets). This gives real-time data on exactly which meals spike your glucose and how severely. This converts a frustrating mystery into a solvable data problem. Founders who use CGMs for even one month typically make lasting dietary changes because the data is impossible to ignore.

Advanced — if crashes persist
Protocol D

Hydration System

For founders under-hydrating or treating hydration as a passive afterthought. Minimal effort, immediate cognitive returns. Duration: 3 days to feel clearly different.

1

500ml water immediately on waking — before anything else

You wake up in a mild state of dehydration every morning after 6–8h without fluid. Brain tissue is 73% water. Even 1% dehydration reduces reaction time, short-term memory, and sustained focus. 500ml immediately on waking corrects overnight deficit before any other input. Put a full water bottle on your bedside table tonight. Drink it before you stand up. This is the simplest, cheapest, most underused performance intervention in this entire framework.

Daily — immediately on waking
2

Structured drinking: 250ml per hour during work

Most people drink reactively — only when thirsty. By the time you're thirsty, you're already at 1–2% dehydration and cognitively impaired. Build a proactive system: a 1L water bottle on the desk, finished by 1pm, refilled once, finished by 5pm. Set a reminder if needed for the first week. After 2 weeks it becomes automatic. The target: clear to light yellow urine. Dark yellow = dehydrated = working at a deficit.

Daily — scheduled input
3

Add electrolytes if working long hours or exercising

Water alone does not maintain electrolyte balance under high cognitive load, sweating, or significant caffeine intake (which is a diuretic). A small amount of sodium + potassium (electrolyte tablet or salt in water) in the morning, especially on high-work days, maintains fluid retention and nerve conductivity. This is not sports performance supplementation — it is basic electrolyte maintenance. Coconut water, sports drink (small amount), or an electrolyte tablet like LMNT or Precision Hydration.

As needed — high-demand days
Protocol E

Eating Window Structure

For founders with completely irregular eating or those wanting to implement time-restricted feeding. This structures when food enters the system, which stabilizes circadian signals and metabolic baseline. This is not a weight-loss protocol.

1

Establish a consistent first and last meal time

The key variable is consistency, not restriction. Eating your first meal at roughly the same time each day — and your last meal at a consistent cutoff — synchronizes your peripheral circadian clocks (gut, liver, pancreas) with your master clock. Irregular meal timing creates metabolic and circadian confusion that compounds sleep and energy problems. This does not require intermittent fasting — just consistent daily anchors. A 8am first meal and 8pm last meal window works for most founders.

Daily anchors — consistent timing
2

Last meal 2–3 hours before sleep target

Eating within 2 hours of sleep raises core body temperature and insulin — both directly disrupt deep sleep onset and quality. This is the most commonly violated rule in this module and one with the clearest physiological mechanism. If you eat at 10pm and sleep at midnight, you are degrading your slow-wave sleep every night. Shift the last meal to 8–9pm for a midnight bedtime, or earlier if possible. The discomfort of changing this habit is real. The sleep improvement within 5 days is also real.

Daily — before sleep window
3

Protein at every meal — especially breakfast

Protein is the most satiety-signaling macronutrient and the primary building block for neurotransmitters including dopamine and serotonin. A protein-rich breakfast (30–40g) stabilizes blood glucose, delays hunger, reduces mid-morning snacking, and provides amino acid precursors for neurotransmitter synthesis that peaks in the morning. Eggs, Greek yogurt, cottage cheese, protein shake, meat. This single breakfast change is one of the most consistently high-rated interventions by founders who implement it.

Daily — every meal
4

Avoid ultra-processed food during work hours

Ultra-processed foods spike blood glucose rapidly, trigger inflammation pathways, and reduce cognitive performance within 30–60 minutes of consumption. This is not about health optimization broadly — it is specifically about not degrading your cognitive state during your most valuable work hours. If you eat ultra-processed food: save it for evenings or weekends, outside your productive window. During work hours, whole food only.

During work hours — strict rule

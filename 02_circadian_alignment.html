Circadian Alignment

Your body runs on a
biological clock.
Yours might be broken.

Inside every cell in your body is a molecular clock. It regulates when cortisol peaks, when body temperature rises, when reaction time is fastest, when deep work is possible. Ignore it and you are working against yourself, at a genetic level, every single day.

Circadian misalignment — the gap between your internal clock and your lived schedule — is one of the most pervasive and least diagnosed performance problems in founders. It doesn't feel like a problem. It just feels like Monday.

Light exposure, feeding timing, social schedules, chronotype — each one is a lever. Most founders have never touched any of them deliberately. This module identifies exactly where your clock is misaligned and gives you a protocol to fix it. The changes are behavioral, not supplemental. They work within 3–7 days. And unlike hacks, they compound.

This is not about becoming a morning person. It's about matching your peak cognitive windows to your most important work — wherever those windows naturally fall.

Training Protocols

Circadian Repair & Optimization

Select the protocol that matches your primary misalignment type. Each is a step-by-step behavioral intervention. No supplements required to start.

Protocol A

Light Anchoring Protocol

Primary intervention for anyone who gets insufficient morning light or uses screens as their first visual input. Effective within 3–5 days. This is the foundational protocol — run it alongside any other.

1

10,000 lux morning light within 30 min of waking

Outdoor natural light is ideal. If living in a low-light context (winter in Seoul, north-facing apartment): use a 10,000 lux light therapy box (e.g. Lumie Vitamin L, Verilux HappyLight). Sit 30–40cm from the box for 10–15 minutes while doing something stationary — eating, reading, planning. Do not stare directly into it. This single action, done consistently, shifts your circadian phase faster than anything else available without medication.

Daily — within 30 min of waking
2

Phone off for the first 30 minutes

Screen light in the morning has two problems: the light signal disrupts natural cortisol rise, and the cognitive content immediately activates your problem-solving mode before your brain has completed its morning reset. No email, no Slack, no news within the first 30 min. Replace with light box + physical activity + a brief anchoring practice (even 3 minutes). This is often the highest-friction item on this list — and the most impactful.

Daily — morning 30 min window
3

Brief outdoor movement in morning light

10–15 min of outdoor walking within 60 min of waking — even overcast sky delivers 10,000–50,000 lux, far more than any indoor light. This stacks circadian reset, adenosine clearance, and movement-triggered BDNF. In winter: wear a coat, go outside anyway. The light comes through cloud cover.

Daily — within 60 min of waking
4

Eliminate bright overhead light after 9pm

The morning light sets the clock forward. Evening bright light delays it. After 9pm: dim, warm-toned light sources only. Use floor lamps, candles, salt lamps. Avoid overhead LED lighting. If you use a computer after 9pm, use blue-light-blocking glasses (physical glasses, not software mode — the evidence for software modes is weak). This preserves your natural melatonin rise and speeds sleep onset.

Daily — after 9pm
Protocol B

Social Jet Lag Repair

For founders with large weekday/weekend sleep timing gaps. If Monday feels like jet lag, this is why. Duration: 21 days to stabilize.

1

Weekend wake time within 1 hour of weekday wake time

This is the primary rule. Non-negotiable. Sleeping 2+ hours later on weekends shifts your circadian clock backward — exactly like flying 2 time zones west every Friday. Monday morning is your recovery. If you need more sleep on weekends, go to bed earlier. Do not change your wake time. Use the morning light protocol on weekends exactly as on weekdays.

Weekly rule — weekends
2

Alcohol management on weekends

Weekend late-night alcohol compounds social jet lag: it both delays sleep timing and degrades quality. If drinking socially, finish 3+ hours before bed and hydrate deliberately (500ml water before sleep). This does not eliminate the performance cost but significantly reduces the Sunday-night sleep disruption that drives Monday impairment.

Weekend evenings
3

Sunday evening pre-set

Sunday night is not the last day of the weekend — it's the setup day for the week. Sunday evening: same wind-down routine as weekdays, same light dimming, same sleep time as Thursday night. The founder who protects Sunday evening adds approximately 30% more effective cognitive hours to Monday and Tuesday. This is one of the highest-leverage schedule decisions available.

Weekly — Sunday evening
Protocol C

Peak Window Scheduling

For founders whose deep work doesn't align with their actual peak cognitive window. This is a scheduling intervention, not a biological one. Results are immediate.

1

Identify your peak window precisely

For 5 days, rate your focus and cognitive sharpness every hour from 7am to 9pm on a simple 1–5 scale. Do this in a notes app or paper. At the end of 5 days, you will have a clear picture of your daily performance curve. Most people have 1–2 genuine peak windows of 2–3 hours each. Everything else is maintenance work.

5-day diagnostic
2

Block your peak window for deep work only — no exceptions

Block your peak 2–3 hours in your calendar as non-negotiable deep work time. Zero meetings. Zero calls. Zero Slack. This window is for strategy, critical thinking, product decisions, investor materials — whatever requires your best cognitive state. If stakeholders need access to you during this window: create a policy. "I'm unavailable 9–12am; I have a standing rule." Protect it like a surgery slot.

Calendar restructure — immediate
3

Schedule low-cognitive tasks in post-peak windows

Email, admin, short calls, routine decisions — these go in your low-performance windows, typically mid-afternoon. This is not procrastination; it is cognitive resource allocation. You are not saving your best mental state for tasks that don't need it. Most founders do this exactly backwards: they check email in the morning peak and do strategy in the afternoon trough.

Daily scheduling principle
4

Strategic nap (optional) — 90-min or 20-min only

If you have a significant afternoon trough: a 20-min nap (before 3pm) or a full 90-min sleep cycle nap can restore afternoon cognitive performance significantly. The 20-min version uses light sleep without entering slow-wave (avoids grogginess). The 90-min version completes a full cycle. Nothing in between — 30–60 min creates sleep inertia without full recovery. Elite performers across sports, military, and medicine use strategic napping deliberately.

As needed — before 3pm
Protocol D

Evening Chronotype Shift

For evening-type founders who need to shift their clock earlier for external obligations. A deliberate, systematic approach — not willpower. Duration: 2–3 weeks for a meaningful shift.

1

Shift gradually — 15 min earlier every 3 days

Attempting a 2-hour wake-time shift overnight fails. The circadian clock shifts at a maximum of about 15–30 min per day. Move your wake time 15 min earlier every 3 days, using morning light exposure to lock in each shift. This takes 2–3 weeks for a 1.5-hour total shift. Slow feels frustrating but is the only approach that works without significant performance degradation during the transition.

Gradual — 15 min per 3 days
2

Bright light immediately on waking at new target time

During the shift period, use a light therapy box at maximum intensity immediately on waking — even before your body wants to be awake. The light signal overrides the internal clock signal and anchors the new wake time. This is the mechanism that makes the gradual shift actually work. Without morning light, the shift fails.

Daily during shift period
3

Eliminate evening bright light and screens aggressively

Shifting earlier requires simultaneously pulling the evening anchor point forward. Implement strict light dimming after 8pm and a hard screen cutoff. The evening type's primary problem is often excessive bright/blue light in the evening that continuously re-delays their clock. Removing it is as important as adding morning light. Both levers need to move together.

Daily — after 8pm
4

Consider low-dose melatonin in the transition

0.5mg melatonin (not the common 5–10mg doses, which are overdoses) taken 5 hours before your new target sleep time can accelerate the phase advance. This is the evidence-based use of melatonin — as a clock-shifting signal, not a sedative. Low dose only: higher doses create dependence and actually blunt the clock-shifting effect. Consult a physician if you have any concerns. Discontinue once the new schedule is stable.

Optional — during transition period
Protocol E

Travel & Jet Lag Protocol

For founders who travel frequently across time zones. Each zone crossed = one day of cognitive impairment if unmanaged. This protocol cuts that to hours.

1

Begin shifting your schedule 2–3 days before departure

For eastward travel: sleep and wake 1 hour earlier per day for 2–3 days before the flight. For westward: shift 1 hour later. This pre-loads the shift so your body is partially adapted before you land. The traveler who does nothing is fully jet-lagged on arrival. The traveler who pre-shifts by 2 hours lands at 50% adaptation and recovers in half the time.

Pre-travel — 2–3 days before
2

Immediate light and schedule adoption on arrival

On landing, immediately adopt local time. Do not nap if it is daytime at destination. Use bright light exposure immediately. Eat your first meal at local meal time. This aggressive immediate adoption accelerates adaptation. The traveler who "just rests" on arrival delays adaptation by 24–48 hours. If you need to perform within 24 hours of landing: stay awake at all costs until local bedtime, use light, eat at local times.

On arrival — immediate
3

Fasting strategy in-flight

Food is a circadian signal. Fasting during a long-haul flight and eating your first meal at local breakfast time at the destination significantly accelerates adaptation. This works because the food signal strongly resets the peripheral clock (gut, liver) even when the master brain clock is still on home time. Practical: skip airline meals, drink water only, eat a real meal when you land (if morning) or at the first local meal time that matches.

In-flight and on arrival
4

Use melatonin at destination bedtime only

0.5mg melatonin at local bedtime for the first 2–3 nights at the destination. Not as a sedative — as a clock signal. The timing matters more than the dose. Taking it at 10pm local time tells your clock it is nighttime now, regardless of what your home-time clock says. Combined with light exposure and meal timing, this is the most complete jet lag intervention stack available.

First 2–3 nights at destination
